Jim Cramer's Mad Money Recap

Earnings Reports from Major Banks

  • JP Morgan (JPM) reported a strong quarter with 18% revenue growth in its commercial and investment bank, driven by a 35% increase in investment banking fees and a 10% increase in consumer banking revenue. The stock rose 7% after the earnings report.
  • Wells Fargo (WFC) missed expectations slightly on the top line but delivered a large bottom-line beat, with investment banking fees up 59% and a $4 billion share buyback. The stock rose 3% after the earnings report.
  • City Group (C) reported a solid quarter with growth in each of its businesses, beating expectations, and announced a $2 billion repurchase program. The stock rose 5% after the earnings report.
  • Bank of America (BAC) had a modest revenue beat and solid earnings, with all four segments growing, but missed expectations in its sales and trading business. The stock rose 2% after the earnings report.

Goldman Sachs Earnings Report

  • Reported a huge revenue beat and earned $11.95 per share, more than doubling its earnings year-over-year. The stock rose 12% after the earnings report.
  • Global banking and markets business saw 33% revenue growth, with fixed income, currencies, and commodities trading up 35%. This was driven by strong client activity and a favorable market environment.
  • Strong expense control, with an efficiency ratio of 59.6%, down from 75% last year. This was driven by cost-cutting initiatives and a focus on operational efficiency.
  • Goldman Sachs also announced a $5 billion share buyback program, which should support the stock price.

Morgan Stanley Earnings Report

  • Reported a big revenue beat and a big earnings beat, with a 47% revenue growth in its institutional securities division. The stock rose 10% after the earnings report.
  • Strong expense control, with an expense ratio of 69%, down from 84% last year. This was driven by cost-cutting initiatives and a focus on operational efficiency.
  • CEO Ted Pick highlighted the company's "momentum across all businesses" and a strong pipeline for mergers and acquisitions.
  • Morgan Stanley also announced a $3 billion share buyback program, which should support the stock price.

Interview with Owen Trip, Co-Founder and CEO of Included Health

  • Included Health is a healthcare services platform that provides personalized healthcare benefits to companies and their employees.
  • The company measures quality and empathy in healthcare and works with companies like Walmart to provide high-quality benefits.
  • Owen Trip discussed the importance of evidence-based care and the need for transparency in healthcare costs.
  • Included Health's platform uses AI and data analytics to provide personalized healthcare recommendations and improve health outcomes.
