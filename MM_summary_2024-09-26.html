Jim Cramer's Mad Money Rally Playbook Segment 1: Handling Rallies
  • Stay disciplined during big up days.
  • Evaluate stocks critically, focusing on weaknesses.
  • Avoid emotional decisions; prioritize risk management.
  • Rebalance portfolio as needed.
Segment 2: Selling Strategy
  • Sell strength, especially during major short-term moves higher.
  • Reevaluate stocks; demand more due to increased expense.
  • Use ranking system (1-4) to grade stocks:
    1. Buy at current price.
    2. Buy on pullback.
    3. Sell at higher price.
    4. Needs more information.
  • Consider tax implications.
Segment 3: Emotional Control
  • Recognize emotional biases.
  • Buy low, sell high; avoid buying high.
  • Selling isn't defeat; it's profit-taking.
  • Stay patient, disciplined.
Segment 4: Preparation
  • Prepare for downturns during upswings.
  • Take profits, cut losses during rallies.
  • Stockpile cash (5-10% of portfolio) for buying opportunities.
  • Diversify across sectors, asset classes.
Segment 5: Cash Management
  • Cash provides flexibility, downside protection.
  • Maintain 5-10% cash allocation.
  • Use rallies to raise cash.
  • Avoid over-investment.
Segment 6: Investment Approach
  • Balance capital preservation and appreciation.
  • Avoid over-trading.
  • Believe in rallies, but take profits.
  • Long-term perspective.
Segment 7: Q&A - IPO Investment
  • Evaluate IPO valuations.
  • Consider lockups, selling pressure.
  • Research management team, financials.
  • Avoid excessive IPO enthusiasm.
Segment 8: Q&A - Earnings Analysis
  • Prefer GAAP earnings.
  • Use non-GAAP cautiously.
  • Analyze revenue growth, margins.
  • Watch for earnings surprises.
Segment 9: Q&A - Buying Strategy
  • Buy below basis.
  • Prioritize downside protection.
  • Art, not science; adapt.
  • Avoid chasing momentum.
Segment 10: Q&A - Technical Indicators
  • Relative Strength Index (RSI) signals overbought/oversold.
  • Use RSI with fundamentals.
  • Monitor moving averages.
  • Avoid over-reliance on indicators.
Final Segment: Investment Philosophy
  • Discipline over emotions.
  • Protect downside, let upside take care.
  • Seek reasonable entry points.
  • Stay informed, adaptable.
